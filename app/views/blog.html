Racksburg Hackathon 0

June 27th, 2014

Who doesn't love a good hackathon, or rather, who doesn't love a good learning opportunity? I saw this hackathon as the perfect time to divorce the other programming projects in my life and focus on a project I've had in my head for some time now: my own personal portfolio.

As the work day wound down I found myself doodling a rough sketch of how I wanted this new blog to look. I had already scaffolded my project out using yeoman (an excellent tool for creating off the ground angular.js projects) and began to dig through the seedling it had spawned. I was stunned at how easy it was to pull together the technologies needed to create and run a web application (grunt, bower, angular.js, node.js, bootstrap, you get the idea..). I was excited.

I spent the first hour of the hackathon eating burgers and discussing project ideas with others in the office. The next hour was spent reading angular and bootstrap docs. I was soon ready to dive in. And dive in I did.
